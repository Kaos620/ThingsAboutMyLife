Future Awaits

Rafael Soares Cardoso

“The Most Challenging always gives the best rewards.”

Ok, my college teacher made me do this web page about my life and my expectations for my professional carrer... That made me think, a lot... Sooo, here I come. I actually have a pretty normal life, of course I had some moments in my life that were “unusual”, but it’s fine, I was born in Araxá MG and stil living here since then. When I was younger (about 2/3 years old) I got pretty sick, almost died because of a bacteria called ‘Staphylococcus aureus’ wich usually is just a normal bacteria, but in some cases can get stronger, causing pneumonia and even stronger symptoms , thats what happend to me, first the pneumonia, then, water in my lungs, had to have a emergency surgery and be interned in the hospital for almost two months. After get out of the hospital I had some sequels, being one of them Asthma (wich I had for almost 9 years but healed thanks to the swimming lessons and training). In Elementary School I suffered a lot of bullying, most because I was short, quiet, asthmatic, used glasses, braces, liked videogames, comics and didn’t know how to play football (the nerd stereotype), that made me have some social skills problems, not having friends, etc. In Middle School I started to do martial arts, beggining with Karate that made me start to have better social skills and being more confident, also moving to another school and having my first friend. In the end of it I had changed a lot, learned to talk more, had some friends and got taller. In the first year of High School I was already a total different person, taller, stronger, confident and still liking videogames, anime, comics and more. Then the quarantine, nothing happend in almost 2 years, just played a lot of videogames, and learned English. And now i’m in College, total new life. I still have some sequels of the bullying I suffered years ago, scared of some things I prefer not to talk about, but I’m happy and greatful for what I learned.

My professional expectations for the future are quite simple. I wanna learn more and more about programming, data language etc. I wanna have a job where I earn a lot o money, being able to travel abroad, buy a good house and give my parents the life they coudn’t have when they were younger. I wanna be the professional that is always improving, learn more than everyone else, always taking with me my own life style, wich is, always trying to choose the hardest options, most people don’t go for it, so if do, I’ll be more capable of things that aren’t usual for others to be.
